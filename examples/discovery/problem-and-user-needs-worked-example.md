# Worked example: Problem statement and user needs catalogue

This fictional example is for a Discovery into helping people renew a Blue Badge. It is not based on a live service team and should be adapted before use.

## Scenario context

The team has been asked to explore whether a better renewal journey could reduce avoidable contact and help eligible people renew before their badge expires. Early stakeholder conversations suggested a solution of "build an online renewal tracker", but the Discovery team has deliberately reframed this as a problem to investigate.

## Problem statement

| Field | Example |
| --- | --- |
| Service or policy area | Renewing a Blue Badge |
| Current brief or request | Create an online reminder and renewal journey for people whose Blue Badge is about to expire. |
| Reframed problem | People who rely on a Blue Badge can lose access to parking concessions because renewal requirements, evidence and timings are unclear. This causes avoidable anxiety for users and avoidable contact for councils. |
| Who is affected | Badge holders, carers, council caseworkers, contact centre staff and health professionals who provide supporting evidence. |
| User outcome | People know when and how to renew, understand what evidence they need, and can continue using the scheme if they remain eligible. |
| Government outcome | Eligible people retain access, councils reduce avoidable contact, and caseworkers receive clearer evidence. |
| Evidence summary | Early interviews with 8 badge holders and 4 council staff; contact centre data showing renewal queries; review of existing renewal letters. |
| Problem boundaries | Includes renewal awareness, eligibility evidence and council processing. Excludes policy changes to eligibility criteria and enforcement of badge misuse. |
| Constraints | Local council ownership of some processes; evidence requirements vary by route; users may have low digital confidence or access needs. |
| Key assumptions | Users receive and understand renewal reminders; councils can make renewal status visible; evidence can be explained more clearly without changing policy. |
| Discovery decision needed | Decide whether there is a viable Alpha to test clearer renewal guidance, reminders and evidence submission patterns. |

## User needs catalogue

| ID | User group | Need | Evidence | Priority | Confidence | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| UN-001 | Badge holder | I need to know when to renew so that I do not lose access to parking concessions. | 6 of 8 users did not know how early to renew; support logs show peaks around expired badges. | High | Medium | Validate with more users who receive paper reminders. |
| UN-002 | Carer | I need to understand what evidence is acceptable so that I can help someone apply without repeated follow-up. | Council staff reported frequent evidence-related contact; 3 carers described uncertainty. | High | Medium | Link to evidence rules and content testing. |
| UN-003 | Caseworker | I need evidence to be complete and relevant so that I can make a decision without unnecessary contact. | Interviews with 4 council staff; review of anonymised rejected applications. | High | Medium | May require better guidance and upload prompts. |
| UN-004 | Badge holder with low digital confidence | I need a non-digital route or support so that I can renew even if I cannot use the online service. | 3 users relied on family or phone support; accessibility and assisted digital risk. | High | Low | Needs targeted research with digitally excluded users. |

## How to adapt this example

- Replace the fictional service and evidence with your own.
- Keep evidence and assumptions separate.
- Use plain language that a stakeholder outside the team can understand.
- Link needs to research notes, analytics, operational data or policy evidence.
