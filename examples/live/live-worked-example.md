# Worked example: Live service review pack

This fictional example continues the Blue Badge renewal scenario. It shows how Live artefacts might support service management and continual improvement.

## Service dashboard extract

| Measure | Target | Current result | Interpretation | Action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Renewal applications with complete evidence | 80% | 74% | Improving but below target. | Prioritise evidence guidance improvements. |
| Renewal-related support contact | 20% reduction | 14% reduction | Moving in the right direction. | Review top contact reasons monthly. |
| Accessibility issues open over 30 days | 0 | 1 | Needs action. | Escalate fix at next service review. |
| Incident count this month | 0 high severity | 0 | Stable. | Continue monitoring. |

## Incident management extract

| Incident type | Severity | Response owner | Communication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Evidence upload unavailable | High | Operations lead | Status page, support script, internal alert. |
| Renewal confirmation email delayed | Medium | Product manager | Support script and user-facing update if delay exceeds 24 hours. |

## Continual improvement plan extract

| Improvement | Evidence | Priority | Owner | Expected outcome |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Improve evidence examples | Dashboard and support contacts | High | Product manager | More complete applications. |
| Add clearer assisted digital route | User feedback | Medium | Service owner | Fewer users blocked by upload issues. |
| Review status messaging | Service review | Medium | Content designer | Fewer avoidable status queries. |

## Live review decision

Continue operating the service, prioritise evidence guidance improvements, and review whether a lightweight status feature is justified after another quarter of support data.
