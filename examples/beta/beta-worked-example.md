# Worked example: Beta readiness pack

This fictional example continues the Blue Badge renewal scenario. It shows how Beta artefacts might support a private beta or public beta readiness decision.

## Beta delivery plan summary

| Field | Example |
| --- | --- |
| Beta goal | Build and test the renewal support journey with real users and council staff. |
| Private beta scope | Reminder content, eligibility route, evidence guidance, upload support and confirmation content. |
| Public beta condition | Accessibility issues resolved, support model agreed, security and privacy risks accepted or mitigated. |
| Key dependencies | Council case management process, content approval, accessibility audit, support training. |

## Release readiness

| Readiness area | Evidence | Status | Decision |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Accessibility | Audit completed; 3 medium issues fixed; 1 low issue accepted with workaround. | Amber | Release with documented fix date. |
| Privacy | Data flow reviewed; privacy notice updated; DPIA linked. | Green | Ready. |
| Security | Threat model reviewed; upload controls tested; residual risks accepted. | Green | Ready. |
| Operations | Support routes agreed; runbook drafted; contact centre briefed. | Amber | Release after support rehearsal. |

## Support model extract

| Support need | Channel | Owner | Escalation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Help understanding evidence | Phone and web guidance | Contact centre | Council caseworker |
| Upload problem | Assisted digital support | Support team | Technical support |
| Renewal status query | Confirmation content and phone support | Contact centre | Council caseworker |

## Beta recommendation

Proceed to private beta with a limited cohort, daily monitoring of support contact, weekly accessibility and defect review, and a clear rollback route to existing renewal guidance.
