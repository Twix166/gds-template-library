# Alpha

Alpha tests whether the team can solve the problem identified in Discovery. Use Alpha to prototype, research, test technical options and decide whether there is a viable Beta.

## Recommended artefacts

| Stage of Alpha | Artefact | What it is used for | How to use it | Referenced in GDS guidance |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Start | [Alpha plan](../../templates/alpha/alpha-plan.md) | Plan hypotheses, activities, team roles and governance. | Keep it focused on learning goals rather than delivery of a finished service. | [Alpha phase](https://www.gov.uk/service-manual/agile-delivery/how-the-alpha-phase-works); [Service Standard point 7](https://www.gov.uk/service-manual/service-standard/point-7-use-agile-ways-of-working). |
| Prototype planning | [Prototype description](../../templates/alpha/prototype-description.md) | Describe what a prototype tests and what is deliberately excluded. | Create one for each significant prototype or iteration. | [Prototyping](https://www.gov.uk/service-manual/design/making-prototypes); [Service Standard point 4](https://www.gov.uk/service-manual/service-standard/point-4-make-the-service-simple-to-use). |
| Research planning | [User research plan](../../templates/alpha/user-research-plan.md) | Plan research questions, methods, participants and ethics. | Link each research question to a decision or hypothesis. | [User research in alpha](https://www.gov.uk/service-manual/user-research/user-research-in-alpha); [Service Standard point 1](https://www.gov.uk/service-manual/service-standard/point-1-understand-user-needs). |

## Suggested sequence

1. Start with Alpha hypotheses and the plan.
2. Prototype the riskiest service, content and technical assumptions first.
3. Run regular user research and record findings after each round.
4. Capture architecture decisions as they are made.
5. Define the MVP only after enough evidence exists.
6. Assemble the evidence pack when the team can explain what was tested, learned and recommended.
