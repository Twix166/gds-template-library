# Purpose

Define support channels, roles, service levels, escalation routes and responsibilities.

# When to use this

Use before users rely on the service and update as support arrangements change.

# Inputs

- Service blueprint
- Operational readiness checklist
- User support insight
- Organisational support policies

# Template

Use this structure to describe how users and staff get help when the service is running.

# Field glossary

Hover over table headers, and some field-name cells, to see short definitions in renderers that support HTML title text.

- **Channel**: The route used by users or staff, such as online, phone, email, post or face to face.
- **Channels**: The support or service channels included, such as online, phone, email, post or face to face.
- **Data source**: Where the data comes from.
- **Escalation route**: The route for passing an issue to the next level of support or decision-making.
- **Field**: The name of the information item to complete.
- **First-line owner**: The team or role that handles the support need first.
- **Handover or escalation trigger**: The condition that means support should be passed to another team or role.
- **Improvement action owner**: The person accountable for acting on support insight.
- **Out of scope**: What is deliberately excluded.
- **Response**: The content to complete for the field.
- **Responsibility**: What the role, team or component is responsible for.
- **Review cadence**: How often the item is reviewed.
- **Role or team**: A role, team or supplier involved in support or operations.
- **Service area**: The part of the service covered by the artefact.
- **Service level**: The expected level or timeliness of support.
- **Support hours**: The times when support is available.
- **Support metric**: A measure of support demand, quality or performance.
- **Support need**: The user or staff need that requires support.
- **Support users**: The users, staff or teams who need support.
