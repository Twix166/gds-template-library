# Purpose

Assess accessibility evidence, risks, issues and remediation actions.

# When to use this

Use during Beta before significant releases and assessment preparation.

# Inputs

- Accessibility audit findings
- User research with disabled users
- Design and content evidence
- Defect register

# Template

Use this structure to assess accessibility evidence, unresolved issues and release risk.

# Quality checklist

- Evidence includes automated, manual and user-based testing where appropriate.
- Issues have owners and dates.
- Known limitations and mitigations are transparent.

# Field glossary

Hover over table headers, and some field-name cells, to see short definitions in renderers that support HTML title text.

- **Assessment date**: The date the accessibility assessment was carried out.
- **Assessor**: The person or supplier carrying out an assessment.
- **Conditions**: Conditions that must be met for the decision, approval or acceptance to remain valid.
- **Decision**: The decision made or needed.
- **Due date**: The date by which an action or condition should be completed.
- **Evidence type**: The kind of evidence reviewed, such as audit, test or research.
- **Field**: The name of the information item to complete.
- **Fix or mitigation**: The correction or workaround planned for an issue.
- **Issue**: A current problem that needs action.
- **Issue ID**: A unique identifier for an accessibility, test or delivery issue.
- **Link**: A URL or reference to evidence, artefact or source.
- **Owner**: The person or role accountable for the item.
- **Rationale**: Why the requirement, decision or approach is needed.
- **Release or phase**: The release, beta stage or phase the assessment relates to.
- **Response**: The content to complete for the field.
- **Result**: The outcome of an assessment, test or measure.
- **Scope**: The boundaries of what is included.
- **Service area assessed**: The part of the service assessed.
- **Severity**: The seriousness of an issue or incident.
- **Standard or criteria used**: The accessibility standard, legal requirement or assessment criteria used.
- **Status**: The current state of the item.
- **User impact**: How the issue affects users.
