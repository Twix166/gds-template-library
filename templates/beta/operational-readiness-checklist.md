# Purpose

Check that the service, team, support model and operational controls are ready for release.

# When to use this

Use before private beta, public beta, live launch or significant operational changes.

# Inputs

- Release plan
- Support model
- Incident management process
- Operational runbook

# Template

Use this structure as a release decision aid. It should point to evidence, not replace it.

# Quality checklist

- Support, monitoring and incident response are covered.
- Open risks are understood by decision makers.
- Evidence is current and linked.

# Field glossary

Hover over table headers, and some field-name cells, to see short definitions in renderers that support HTML title text.

- **Area**: The part of the service, organisation or delivery context this row relates to.
- **Condition**: A requirement that must be met before a release or decision can proceed.
- **Criteria**: The condition or standard used to judge readiness.
- **Decision owner**: The person accountable for making or confirming the decision.
- **Due date**: The date by which an action or condition should be completed.
- **Evidence**: The research, data, observation or source that supports the statement.
- **Evidence needed**: The evidence required to validate the hypothesis, assumption or decision.
- **Field**: The name of the information item to complete.
- **Owner**: The person or role accountable for the item.
- **Readiness review date**: The date of the readiness review.
- **Recommended decision**: The proposed decision based on evidence.
- **Release or transition**: The release or service transition being assessed.
- **Response**: The content to complete for the field.
- **Risk**: An uncertain event or condition that could affect outcomes.
- **Status**: The current state of the item.
