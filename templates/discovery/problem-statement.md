# Purpose

Define the problem the team is exploring, the people affected and the outcomes the service should support.

# When to use this

Use during Discovery and revisit whenever the understanding of the problem changes.

# Inputs

- User research findings
- Policy or operational context
- Existing service data
- Stakeholder input

# Quality checklist

- The problem is framed from user and service outcomes, not a preferred solution.
- Evidence and assumptions are clearly separated.
- Scope and exclusions are explicit.

# Field glossary

Hover over table headers, and some field-name cells, to see short definitions in renderers that support HTML title text.

- **Action**: The specific follow-up task needed to progress, validate or close an item.
- **Confidence**: How strong the evidence is: low, medium or high.
- **Current brief or request**: The initial ask, commission or solution request before Discovery reframing.
- **Current pain point**: A difficulty users or staff experience in the current service.
- **Current situation**: A short description of how things work or feel today.
- **Date and version**: The document date and version so readers know how current it is.
- **Discovery team**: The people and roles contributing to the Discovery.
- **Evidence link**: A link or reference to the evidence source.
- **Field**: The name of the information item to complete.
- **How we might measure it**: Possible indicators that would show whether the outcome is being met.
- **Impact on government or operations**: The effect on departments, public bodies, staff, cost, risk or service operations.
- **Impact on users**: The effect the problem has on users.
- **In scope**: What the Discovery or artefact includes.
- **Out of scope**: What the Discovery or artefact deliberately excludes.
- **Outcome**: The result or change the team wants to achieve.
- **Outcome type**: The category of outcome, such as user, operational or policy.
- **Problem users experience**: The problem from the perspective of users.
- **Reason**: Why the item exists, changed or is included.
- **Reframed problem statement**: The problem restated in user and service terms rather than as a solution.
- **Response**: The content to complete for the field.
- **Service or policy area**: The service, policy area or problem space covered by the artefact.
- **Source or owner**: Where the statement came from, or who owns it.
- **Sponsor or accountable owner**: The senior person accountable for the Discovery or service area.
- **Statement**: The evidence, assumption or question being recorded.
- **Type**: The category of item being recorded.
- **User or group**: The user, user group or other affected group.
- **What they are trying to do**: The task, goal or outcome the user wants to achieve.
