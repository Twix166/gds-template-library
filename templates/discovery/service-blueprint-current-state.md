# Purpose

Map how the current service works across user actions, channels, processes, teams and systems.

# When to use this

Use when the team needs to understand the existing service before designing changes.

# Inputs

- Journey maps
- Process maps
- Operational interviews
- System information

# Template

Copy this structure. A current-state blueprint should show how the service works today across channels, teams, systems and policies.

# Quality checklist

- The blueprint shows both user-facing and internal activity.
- Manual workarounds and failure demand are captured.
- Evidence sources are linked.

# Field glossary

Hover over table headers, and some field-name cells, to see short definitions in renderers that support HTML title text.

- **Back-stage activity**: Internal work that happens away from the user but enables the service step.
- **Cause**: The underlying reason a pain point, risk or issue occurs.
- **Channels included**: The channels included in the scope of the artefact.
- **Dependency or constraint**: An external need, limitation or dependency that affects the work.
- **End point**: Where the journey, blueprint or scenario stops.
- **Evidence**: The research, data, observation or source that supports the statement.
- **Evidence used**: The evidence sources used to create the artefact.
- **Fail points**: Places where the current service can break down or create avoidable effort.
- **Field**: The name of the information item to complete.
- **Follow-up action**: The action needed to investigate, resolve or progress the item.
- **Front-stage service touchpoints**: The visible interactions users have with the service.
- **Hard or soft constraint**: Whether the constraint is fixed or potentially negotiable.
- **Impact**: The effect on users, operations, policy, delivery or technology.
- **Impact on service**: How the dependency or constraint affects the service or delivery plan.
- **Opportunity**: A possible area for improvement, not yet a committed solution.
- **Owner**: The person or role accountable for the item.
- **Pain point**: A problem, frustration or avoidable burden in the current service.
- **Policies or rules**: The policy, legal, operational or business rules shaping the service.
- **Response**: The content to complete for the field.
- **Stage**: A step or phase in the user journey or service process.
- **Start point**: Where the journey, blueprint or scenario begins.
- **Support processes**: Internal processes that enable or support the user-facing service.
- **Systems and data**: Systems, data stores, data flows or integrations involved.
- **Type**: The category of item being recorded.
- **User actions**: What users do at this stage of the journey.
- **User group or scenario**: The user group or scenario represented by the blueprint.
- **Who is affected**: The users, staff, teams or organisations affected.
