# Purpose

Provide operational procedures for common support, maintenance and recovery tasks.

# When to use this

Use when the service is moving into Live operations or when operational procedures change.

# Inputs

- Architecture overview
- Support model
- Incident management process
- Monitoring and alerting information

# Template

Use this structure for repeatable operational procedures. Keep steps clear enough for the intended support role.

- Risks and escalation points are explicit.

# Field glossary

Hover over table headers, and some field-name cells, to see short definitions in renderers that support HTML title text.

- **Action**: The specific follow-up task needed to progress, validate or close an item.
- **Change made**: What changed in this version or review.
- **Date**: The date the item was recorded or changed.
- **Escalation owner**: The person or role to contact when the procedure cannot be completed.
- **Escalation point**: When or where to escalate if the step fails.
- **Evidence of completion**: Proof that the operational step has been completed.
- **Expected result**: What should happen after the step is completed.
- **Field**: The name of the information item to complete.
- **Intended user**: The role or person expected to use the runbook.
- **Last tested**: The last date the procedure was tested or rehearsed.
- **Procedure name**: The name of the operational procedure.
- **Reason**: Why the item is included, excluded, changed or recommended.
- **Response**: The content to complete for the field.
- **Reviewer**: The person who reviewed the procedure or artefact.
- **Risk**: An uncertain event or condition that could affect outcomes.
- **Rollback or recovery action**: The action used to recover or return to a known safe state.
- **Service area**: The part of the service covered by the artefact.
- **Step**: A numbered step in a procedure or process.
- **Trigger**: The event or condition that starts a procedure.
- **When to use this procedure**: The situation or trigger where this procedure should be used.
