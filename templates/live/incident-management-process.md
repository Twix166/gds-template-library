# Purpose

Define how incidents are detected, assessed, managed, communicated and reviewed.

# When to use this

Use before launch and keep updated throughout Live operations.

# Inputs

- Support model
- Operational runbook
- Monitoring arrangements
- Organisational incident policy

# Field glossary

Hover over table headers, and some field-name cells, to see short definitions in renderers that support HTML title text.

- **Action**: The specific follow-up task needed to progress, validate or close an item.
- **Communication channels**: The routes used to communicate incident or service information.
- **Communication requirement**: Who must be informed, by when, and through which channel.
- **Decision owner**: The person accountable for making or confirming the decision.
- **Definition**: A precise explanation of what the measure or severity means.
- **Due date**: The date by which an action or condition should be completed.
- **Evidence or output**: The output produced or evidence recorded by the activity.
- **Field**: The name of the information item to complete.
- **Improvement action**: The action agreed to reduce recurrence or improve the service.
- **Incident**: An event that disrupts, degrades or risks the service.
- **Incident owner**: The role accountable for managing incidents.
- **Monitoring sources**: The alerts, dashboards, logs or reports used to detect issues.
- **Owner**: The person or role accountable for the item.
- **Post-incident review owner**: The person accountable for arranging and completing the post-incident review.
- **Response**: The content to complete for the field.
- **Response time**: The expected time to respond to an incident.
- **Root cause**: The underlying cause of the incident or issue.
- **Service name**: The name of the service being documented.
- **Severity**: The seriousness of an issue or incident.
- **Step**: A numbered step in a procedure or process.
