# Purpose

Plan Alpha objectives, scope, activities, governance and delivery cadence.

# When to use this

Use at the start of Alpha and update during planning reviews.

# Inputs

- Discovery recommendations
- Team roles
- Research and prototyping goals
- Risks and dependencies

# Template

Copy this structure to plan Alpha around learning, not delivery of a production service.

# Quality checklist

- Alpha is focused on learning, not full delivery.
- Activities are linked to hypotheses.
- Governance and decision points are clear.

# Field glossary

Hover over table headers, and some field-name cells, to see short definitions in renderers that support HTML title text.

- **Activity**: A release, delivery or assurance activity that needs to happen.
- **Alpha goal**: The main learning outcome the Alpha is intended to achieve.
- **Area**: The part of the service, organisation or delivery context this row relates to.
- **Decision needed at end of Alpha**: The decision the team needs to make when Alpha finishes.
- **Decision or playback**: A decision point or show-and-tell where learning is shared.
- **Detail**: The specific information needed for this area.
- **Discovery evidence used**: Discovery findings, data or artefacts used as input.
- **Evidence needed**: The evidence required to validate the hypothesis, assumption or decision.
- **Field**: The name of the information item to complete.
- **How to test**: The method the team will use to validate or invalidate the hypothesis.
- **Hypothesis or assumption**: A testable belief about users, service design, technology or operations.
- **ID**: A stable identifier used to track the item across documents.
- **In scope**: What is included in the scope.
- **Out of scope**: What is deliberately excluded.
- **Output**: The artefact, evidence or decision produced by the activity.
- **Owner**: The person or role accountable for the item.
- **Reason**: Why the item is included, excluded, changed or recommended.
- **Response**: The content to complete for the field.
- **Review cadence**: How often the item is reviewed.
- **Service or problem area**: The service, policy area or problem space covered by the artefact.
- **Start and end dates**: The planned start and end dates.
- **Status**: The current state of the item.
- **Week**: The Alpha week or timebox for the activity.
- **Why it matters**: Why the item is important for users, service outcomes or decisions.
