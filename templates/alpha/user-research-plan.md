# Purpose

Plan user research questions, participants, methods, ethics and outputs.

# When to use this

Use before each research round or as an overall Alpha research plan.

# Inputs

- User needs catalogue
- Prototype description
- Recruitment requirements
- Research ethics guidance

# Template

Copy this structure before each research round. Link every research question to a decision the team needs to make.

# Quality checklist

- Research questions are linked to decisions.
- Participant criteria include relevant access needs.
- Ethical and privacy considerations are covered.

# Field glossary

Hover over table headers, and some field-name cells, to see short definitions in renderers that support HTML title text.

- **Access or support needs**: Access needs, support needs or adjustments to consider.
- **Area**: The part of the service, organisation or delivery context this row relates to.
- **Dates**: The dates covered by the activity.
- **Decisions this research supports**: The team decisions that the research is intended to inform.
- **Evidence needed**: The evidence required to validate the hypothesis, assumption or decision.
- **Field**: The name of the information item to complete.
- **ID**: A stable identifier used to track the item across documents.
- **Method**: The research or test method used.
- **Owner**: The person or role accountable for the item.
- **Participant group**: A group of research participants with shared characteristics.
- **Plan**: The planned approach for the area.
- **Prototype or artefact tested**: The prototype, document or service artefact included in research.
- **Recruitment notes**: Notes about how participants will be found and invited.
- **Related hypothesis**: The hypothesis this research question or test relates to.
- **Research lead**: The person responsible for the research round.
- **Research question**: The question the research is intended to answer.
- **Research round**: The research round, sprint or study the finding relates to.
- **Response**: The content to complete for the field.
- **Target number**: The intended number of participants.
- **Why included**: Why this participant group, activity or item is included.
