# Purpose

Summarise findings from user testing and the implications for service design.

# When to use this

Use after each prototype test, usability test or research round.

# Inputs

- Research notes
- Session recordings or transcripts
- Prototype description
- Observation notes

# Template

Use this structure after each prototype test or research round. Keep raw observations separate from interpretation and decisions.

# Field glossary

Hover over table headers, and some field-name cells, to see short definitions in renderers that support HTML title text.

- **Action**: The specific follow-up task needed to progress, validate or close an item.
- **Backlog item or decision link**: A link to the backlog item, decision record or action created from the finding.
- **Confidence**: How strong the evidence is: low, medium or high.
- **Dates**: The dates covered by the activity.
- **Decision**: The decision made or needed.
- **Due date**: The date by which an action or condition should be completed.
- **Evidence**: The research, data, observation or source that supports the statement.
- **Example evidence**: A concise example or link showing the evidence behind the pattern.
- **Field**: The name of the information item to complete.
- **Finding**: A synthesised insight from evidence, not a single raw observation.
- **ID**: A stable identifier used to track the item across documents.
- **Owner**: The person or role accountable for the item.
- **Participant groups affected**: The participant groups who experienced or demonstrated the pattern.
- **Participants**: The users, staff or participants included in research.
- **Pattern observed**: A recurring behaviour, issue or insight seen across research.
- **Prototype or artefact tested**: The prototype, document or service artefact included in research.
- **Recommendation**: The proposed action or decision based on the finding.
- **Research questions covered**: The research questions addressed in the findings.
- **Research round**: The research round, sprint or study the finding relates to.
- **Response**: The content to complete for the field.
- **Severity or importance**: How serious or decision-relevant the finding is.
