# Purpose

Describe what a prototype is intended to test and how it supports learning.

# When to use this

Use for each significant prototype or prototype iteration in Alpha.

# Inputs

- Hypotheses
- User needs
- Journey maps
- Research plan

# Template

Copy this structure for each significant prototype or prototype iteration.

# Quality checklist

- The prototype has clear research questions.
- It is only as detailed as needed to learn.
- Known limitations are documented.

# Field glossary

Hover over table headers, and some field-name cells, to see short definitions in renderers that support HTML title text.

- **Area**: The part of the service, organisation or delivery context this row relates to.
- **Current design choice**: The design approach currently being tested or documented.
- **Evidence or rationale**: The evidence or reasoning behind the design choice.
- **Excluded from prototype**: What the prototype deliberately does not include.
- **Fidelity**: How realistic or detailed the prototype is.
- **Field**: The name of the information item to complete.
- **Hypotheses tested**: The hypotheses this prototype or activity is intended to test.
- **Included in prototype**: What the prototype includes.
- **Link to prototype**: A link to the prototype, if shareable.
- **Open question**: A question that still needs evidence or a decision.
- **Participant group**: A group of research participants with shared characteristics.
- **Prototype name**: The name or short label for the prototype.
- **Prototype version**: The prototype version being tested.
- **Reason**: Why the item is included, excluded, changed or recommended.
- **Research question**: The question the research is intended to answer.
- **Response**: The content to complete for the field.
- **Success or learning signal**: What would show success or create useful learning.
- **Task or scenario**: The realistic task or scenario used for testing.
- **User journey or scenario**: The journey or scenario represented by the prototype.
